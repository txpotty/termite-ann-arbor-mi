Comprehensive Termite Control Services

Our termite control service is far more than just spraying for bugs. It’s a multi-faceted approach designed for maximum effectiveness and long-term protection. We take every step seriously, from the initial consultation to preventative maintenance.

Detailed Property Inspection

The foundation of effective termite control is a thorough inspection. Our certified technicians meticulously examine your property, both interior and exterior, to identify signs of termite activity. This includes looking for mud tubes, damaged wood, and listening for the tell-tale sounds of a termite colony. We also assess potential entry points and conducive conditions that might be attracting termites.

Identification of Termite Species

Knowing the specific type of termite infesting your property is crucial. Different termite species require different treatment protocols. Drywood termites, subterranean termites, and dampwood termites each have unique behaviors and nesting habits. Our experts are trained to accurately identify the species, ensuring the most appropriate and effective treatment plan is developed.

Assessment of Infestation Severity

We determine the extent of the infestation, understanding how widespread the colony is and the level of damage already incurred. This assessment helps us tailor the treatment plan to the specific needs of your property, ensuring that we address the entire problem, not just the visible symptoms.

Targeted Treatment Strategies

Based on the inspection findings, we develop a customized treatment plan. We utilize a combination of proven methods to eliminate active colonies and prevent future infestations. Our treatments are selected for their effectiveness and safety, considering the specific conditions of your property and any environmental concerns.

Liquid Termite Barriers

Creating a protective barrier around your property is a common and effective method for controlling subterranean termites. We apply professional-grade liquid termiticides to the soil around your foundation, creating a treated zone that termites cannot cross. This not only eliminates active termites attempting to enter but also prevents new colonies from establishing themselves.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer a discreet and environmentally friendly alternative or supplement to liquid treatments. Bait stations are strategically placed around your property. Termites forage on the bait, which contains a slow-acting active ingredient. This ingredient is then carried back to the colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony, including the queen.

Direct Wood Treatments

For localized infestations or to protect specific wooden structures, we may apply direct wood treatments. These treatments can include termiticidal foams or liquids injected directly into infested wood or applied to surfaces vulnerable to attack. This offers targeted protection where it’s needed most.

Preventative Measures and Ongoing Monitoring

Effective termite control isn’t just about eliminating existing problems; it’s also about preventing them from returning. We provide recommendations and implement strategies to minimize the risk of future infestations.

Addressing Conducive Conditions

Termites are attracted to moisture and wood-to-soil contact. We advise on and can assist with addressing conducive conditions such as leaking pipes, poor drainage, and wood debris around the foundation. Minimizing these attractants makes your property less appealing to termites.

Follow-Up Inspections and Monitoring

Our commitment extends beyond the initial treatment. We offer follow-up inspections and monitoring services to ensure that the treatment has been successful and to detect any new termite activity early on. This ongoing vigilance provides long-term protection for your investment.

Signs You Need Pest Control Services in Ann Arbor

Identifying a pest problem early can save you significant time, money, and stress. While some pests are obvious, others, like termites, can be incredibly subtle in their presence. Recognizing the signs of an infestation is the first crucial step towards effective pest management. Regardless of the type of pest, from the smallest ant to the most destructive termite, noticing the early indicators allows for prompt action and minimizes potential damage and health risks. Knowing what to look for empowers you to protect your home and family proactively. Don’t wait until the problem is overwhelming; a little vigilance goes a long way in maintaining a pest-free environment. If you observe any of these warning signs, it’s a clear indication that a professional pest inspection in Ann Arbor is warranted. Our team is trained to identify even the most subtle clues that point to pest activity, providing you with a comprehensive assessment and the right solutions.

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Mud tubes are one of the most common and definitive signs of subterranean termites. These pencil-sized tunnels, constructed from soil and wood particles, allow termites to travel discreetly between their underground colony and their food source (your home’s wooden structure). Finding these tubes on your foundation walls, crawl spaces, or even interior walls is a strong indicator of termite presence and active feeding. They serve as protected highways, shielding termites from predators and dry air. If you spot these, do not disturb them, as this can cause the termites to move to another, less visible area. Instead, contact us immediately for a professional inspection. Identifying the location of these tubes helps our experts trace the path of the infestation and target treatment precisely where it is needed most. Ignoring mud tubes can lead to significant structural damage over time, as the termite colony continues to feast on your home.

Damaged Wood (Hollowed or Discolored)

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. This means that visible external damage might not be immediately apparent. However, if you tap on areas of wood and they sound hollow, or if you notice blistering or discoloration of paint or wallpaper, it could be a sign that termites are active beneath the surface. Soft spots in wooden flooring or structural beams are also red flags. This damage often progresses unnoticed until a significant portion of the wood is compromised, highlighting the insidious nature of termite infestations. Professional inspection can identify this internal damage even when it’s not outwardly obvious, using tools like moisture meters and sounding devices. Early detection of damaged wood allows for localized treatment and repair before the structural integrity of your home is severely impacted.

Discarded Termite Wings

Termite swarmers, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that emerge from mature colonies to establish new ones. After they swarm, they shed their wings near windowsills, doors, or other light sources. Finding piles of small, translucent wings resembles fish scales is a definite sign that a termite colony is nearby and potentially infesting your property. Swarming usually occurs during warmer months, often after rainfall. While the swarm itself might be brief, the discarded wings are evidence of a significant, established colony. Don’t mistake these for ant wings; termite wings are typically all the same size, while ant wings are different sizes. Discovering these wings is a critical indicator that you need to act fast to prevent or address an active infestation. A professional inspection can locate the entry points where these swarmers emerged, pinpointing the location of the primary colony.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites produce small, pellet-like fecal droppings called frass. These droppings are often pushed out of small holes near infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood being consumed. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or coffee grounds near wooden structures is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites don’t require contact with soil, making their infestations more difficult to detect initially. Frass accumulation is often one of the first visible signs. The presence of frass helps our technicians determine the location of drywood termite colonies and target treatment directly to the infested wood. Cleaning up the frass doesn’t solve the problem; the colony will continue to expel more. Prompt professional intervention is necessary to eliminate the colony and prevent further damage.

Clicking Sounds in Walls

Soldier termites, the defenders of the colony, bang their heads against the wood or shake their bodies when the colony is disturbed. This behavior creates a faint clicking or tapping sound that may be audible within walls, particularly in quiet areas or at night. Hearing these noises is a less common but significant sign of active termite infestation within the structures of your home. It indicates a substantial presence within the walls. While not as definitive as finding mud tubes or wings, combined with other signs, clicking sounds can strengthen the suspicion of a termite problem. Our experienced technicians can use specialized listening equipment to confirm the source of these sounds and pinpoint the location of the active colony within your walls. Don’t dismiss unusual sounds coming from within your walls, especially if you’ve noticed other potential signs of termites.

Visible Termites

Occasionally, you might see active termites, although they typically avoid light. Swarmers emerging from wood are the most commonly seen form. Less often, you might spot worker termites, which are small, cream-colored insects, if you break open damaged wood or disturb an infested area. Seeing live termites is, of course, the most direct evidence of an active infestation and requires immediate action. Don’t attempt to handle or treat visible termites yourself. Their presence indicates a much larger problem within the structure of your home or business. Contacting a pest control professional as soon as you see live termites is crucial for stopping the infestation before it causes further damage. Our experts can accurately identify the type of termite you are seeing, which is essential for implementing the correct treatment strategy.

Bubbling or Uneven Paint

Termites can create tunnels and voids behind paint or wallpaper, causing the surface to appear uneven, bubbling, or peeling. This is often mistaken for water damage, but upon closer inspection, there might be no signs of moisture. This cosmetic damage can be an early indicator of termite activity beneath the surface, as they consume the wood and cause the outer layers to become unstable. It’s a subtle sign that is often overlooked but can point to an active infestation. If you notice bubbling or peeling paint that doesn’t seem related to moisture, it’s wise to have a professional inspect for termites. Catching this early can prevent more extensive damage and costly repairs down the line.

Stiff Doors or Windows

As termites consume wooden door and window frames, they can cause the frames to warp and become misshapen. This can make it difficult to open or close doors and windows. This symptom is often attributed to humidity or settling, but it can be a sign of significant termite damage within the frame itself. If you experience sudden difficulty operating a door or window, especially if there are no other obvious causes like humidity swelling, it’s worth considering the possibility of a termite issue. A professional inspection can determine if termites are the culprit behind your stiff doors or windows, allowing for targeted treatment and repair. Addressing this problem early can prevent the need for costly frame replacement.

Beyond the clear indicators of termite activity, it’s important to be aware of the broader signs that suggest you might need general pest control services in your Ann Arbor property. Pests come in all shapes and sizes, from crawling insects to rodents, and each type can present unique challenges and health risks. Recognizing these signs early on is key to preventing minor nuisances from escalating into full-blown infestations that can impact your health, safety, and property value. Ann Arbor’s diverse climate means a variety of pests are common throughout the year, requiring varied approaches to control. Being proactive about pest management protects your living or working environment and offers peace of mind. If you notice any of the following issues, it’s a good time to call in the professionals for a thorough assessment and customized treatment plan.

Increased Activity of Pests (Indoors or Outdoors)

A noticeable increase in the number of pests you see, whether inside your home or around your property, is a strong indicator of an infestation. This could be ants marching in lines, spiders appearing more frequently, or spiders spinning webs in new locations. While a single pest might not be cause for concern, a sudden surge in their numbers suggests they are breeding and establishing a colony nearby. Different pests become more active during certain seasons, so understanding normal seasonal fluctuations is important. However, if you notice an unusual or significant increase in pest activity outside of these norml patterns, it’s a sign that professional intervention might be needed. Monitoring pest activity around your home helps you catch developing problems early, allowing for more effective and less invasive treatment.

Droppings or Urine Stains

Rodent infestations are often made obvious by the presence of droppings and urine stains. These can be found in attics, basements, kitchens, pantries, and along walls and pathways where rodents travel. Rodent droppings are typically small, dark pellets. Urine stains may be accompanied by a noticeable, unpleasant odor. These are clear signs of rodent presence and indicate a potential health hazard, as rodent waste can carry diseases. Identifying the location and size of droppings can also help determine the type of rodent and the severity of the infestation. Professional pest control can safely and effectively remove rodents, sanitize affected areas, and implement preventative measures to keep them from returning. Don’t underestimate the health risks associated with rodent infestations; prompt action is essential.

Gnaw Marks on Food Packaging or Structures

Rodents, like mice and rats, constantly gnaw to keep their teeth trimmed. They will chew on a variety of materials, including food packaging, electrical wires, wood, and even plastic pipes. Finding gnaw marks on food boxes in your pantry or on baseboards and furniture is a clear sign of rodent activity. Gnawing on electrical wires poses a significant fire hazard. The size and shape of the gnaw marks can sometimes help identify the type of rodent. If you observe these signs, it’s crucial to address the issue promptly to prevent damage to your property and mitigate fire risks. Professional rodent control can eliminate the infestation and help you identify and seal entry points to prevent future problems.

Unusual Odors

Pest infestations can often produce distinct and unpleasant odors. A musty or oily smell can indicate a rodent infestation. A sweet, sickly smell might point to a large number of cockroaches. Dead pests can also produce foul odors as they decompose. If you notice unusual or persistent odors in your home that you can’t explain, it could be a sign of a hidden pest problem. These odors can be particularly strong in enclosed spaces like attics, crawl spaces, or behind walls. Our experienced technicians are trained to identify the source of these odors, leading to the discovery of potentially hidden pest infestations. Don’t ignore persistent strange smells; they are often a warning sign that something is amiss.

Nesting Materials or Harborage Sites

Pests, especially rodents and some insects, will gather materials to build nests or find sheltered areas to hide and breed. Finding shredded paper, fabric, insulation, or other soft materials in secluded areas like attics, walls, or behind appliances is a sign that pests are nesting. Identifying these nesting sites helps pest control professionals target their treatments directly to the source of the infestation. Common harborage sites include cluttered areas, basements, garages, and outdoor sheds. Keeping your property tidy and free of clutter can help reduce the likelihood of pests finding suitable nesting sites. If you discover what appears to be a pest nest, avoid disturbing it and contact a professional for safe and effective removal and treatment.

Damage to Fabric or Belongings

Certain pests, such as moths, carpet beetles, and silverfish, feed on fabrics and organic materials. Finding irregular holes or damage to clothing, carpets, upholstery, or stored items can be a sign of an infestation. This type of damage is often localized and can progress unnoticed until significant items are affected. Identifying the type of damage can help pinpoint the specific pest responsible. Protecting stored items in airtight containers and regularly inspecting vulnerable materials can help prevent such damage. If you discover unexplained damage to your fabrics or belongings, a professional pest inspection can identify the culprit and recommend the appropriate treatment. Early detection of fabric pests can save valuable items from destruction.

Noises in Walls or Ceilings

Scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds coming from within your walls or ceilings are often indicative of rodent activity. You might hear these noises more at night when rodents are most active. Bird or squirrel activity in attics can also create noise. Identifying the type of sound and when you hear it can provide clues about the type of pest present. These noises can be unnerving and a clear sign that you have unwanted guests in your home’s structure. Our experts are adept at identifying the source of these noises and implementing humane and effective removal strategies. Don’t let noises in your walls or ceilings go unaddressed; they often signal an active infestation that requires professional attention.

Seeing Pests During the Day

Many pests are nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night. Seeing pests like cockroaches or rodents during the day can indicate a large infestation, as the population has grown to the point where they are forced to forage for food and water even during daylight hours. It could also suggest overcrowding or a disruption to their normal habitat. The presence of typically nocturnal pests during the day is a significant warning sign that necessitates prompt professional intervention. It suggests the infestation is well-established and likely widespread. Our team can conduct a thorough inspection to determine the extent of the problem and develop a comprehensive treatment plan.

Small Piles of Wood Shavings or Sawdust

While termites are known for damaging wood, carpenter ants also tunnel through wood to create nests, leaving behind piles of wood shavings that resemble sawdust, called frass. Unlike termites, carpenter ants don’t eat the wood; they simply excavate it. Finding these piles near wooden structures, windowsills, or doorframes is a sign of potential carpenter ant activity. The presence of frass helps distinguish between a termite and a carpenter ant infestation, which requires different treatment methods. Our experts can identify the pest responsible for the wood damage and implement the appropriate control measures. Don’t ignore small piles of sawdust; they are a clear indicator of a wood-destroying pest.

Grease Marks or Smudges Along Walls

Rodents, particularly rats and mice, have oily fur and often travel along established routes, typically close to walls and baseboards. Over time, the grease and dirt from their fur can leave visible smudge marks along these pathways. These marks are often found in areas of high rodent traffic and can help identify the rodents’ entry points and travel routes. Finding these grease marks is a strong indicator of a recurring rodent problem and suggests that they have established pathways within your home. Our team can follow these trails to locate nesting sites and entry points, allowing for effective treatment and prevention. Identifying these subtle signs is a crucial part of a comprehensive pest control strategy.

Pet Behavior Changes

Pets can often detect pest activity before humans do. If your pet is suddenly scratching excessively, agitated, constantly sniffing or pawing at certain areas, or exhibiting unusual behavior, it could be a sign of pests like fleas, ticks, or even rodents. Pets might also become fixated on certain areas where pests are hiding. Paying attention to changes in your pet’s behavior can provide an early warning signal of a potential pest problem. If your pet seems unusually agitated or focused on a specific area, investigate further and consider a professional pest inspection. Your pet’s behavior might be the first clue to a hidden infestation affecting not only your home but also their well-being.
